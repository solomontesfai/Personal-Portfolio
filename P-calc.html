Calculator

One of the first but fundamental programming skills that I was able to learn would be prototyping. Prototyping extends and becomes a structure on what you plan to make. Prototyping is what shines light and expands on ideas. This calculator assignment became a main basis for that. This assignment introduced the methods of JavaScript and how it can be used.

At the start of every website, I was always taught to present a draft or prototype of the functions of the website. This prototype would also illustrate the design and purpose. Iterating became a major role in the development of any project. It allowed things to remain organized and made room for personal goals/progressions.

Sites/softwares I used that were provided would be Figma and Canva. Figma is a wonderful site that provides me many preset dimensions, colors, and flexibility. Canva is an amazing site to provide templates for slide presentations and mood boards. These websites became wonderful sources in my CTE Web Application class.

The first step in prototyping would be research. I would be tasked to go to different websites and take inspiration from them. If I was making a travel website, I looked at travel websites and what info was provided to users. Studying other websites is a crucial part of website development. It allows programmers to know exactly what to include, detail, and take out if necessary. After finding a layout and what to include, we would be tasked with creating mood boards to find a theme for the website. Generally, you’d want to find neutral vibrant colors that are modernistic. Of course all websites are different. But we were mainly tasked with creating websites for young adults. The last step would be image searching. There were many royalty free websites that were able to be referenced. Images were chosen based on idea and theme. A contrasting image would not make the website look nice. The prototyping process took the CART Habit of Mind “Investigate.” Jumping from site to site and taking in info is all part of the prototyping process.
